Nuestra Misión · Why we cook

After many hours spent helping members of the nearest food shelter, Sunday Friends, receive fresh groceries, my charity group and I felt there was more hospitality, enrichment, and flavor to give to the Sunday Friends experience. Conversations with the organization's manager led to the birth of our cooking class, Community Tables, where members pile in for fresh ingredients and my favorite recipes.

Our classes were first held in an elementary school cafeteria, where members and instructors cooked side by side over everything from salads to spring rolls, each class followed by a brief course on nutrition. Then, when Sunday Friends later moved to the Sobrato NPO campus, we were able to upgrade to more advanced recipes and open our classes to even more members. Now, stories in English, Vietnamese, and Spanish alike are shared as members stir and simmer together: individuals coming together over food, hospitality, and friendship.

— Kimi Yashar, founder
